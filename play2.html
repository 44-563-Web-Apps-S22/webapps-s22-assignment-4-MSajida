The Miser

Act III : The Act is between Valere and Master Jacques

JAC .

I know it very well.

VAL.

And that you don't know me yet?

JAC.

I beg your pardon.

VAL.

You will beat me, you say?

JAC.

I only spoke in jest.

VAL.

I don't like your jesting, and (_beating_ JACQUES) remember that you are but a sorry hand at it.

JAC.

(_alone_). Plague take all sincerity; it is a bad trade. I give it up for the future, and will cease to tell the truth. It is all very well for my master to beat me; but as for that Mr. Steward, what right has he to do it? I will be revenged on him if I can.
